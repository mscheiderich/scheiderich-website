The Atlanta Driving Reality

One of the Most Dangerous Driving Cities in America

Atlanta has been ranked the worst city in the United States for driving in multiple independent studies. A 2024 analysis by Conboy Law, which evaluated traffic fatality rates, vehicle density, and commute delays across 36 major U.S. cities, gave Atlanta a perfect 100 out of 100 on its Driving Risk Score — the worst possible result — placing it ahead of Los Angeles. A separate study by Lance Surety Bonds found Atlanta drivers experience 3.86 accidents per 1,000 drivers and 20.6 motor vehicle deaths per 100,000 residents.

The numbers from Georgia's own data are just as striking. According to Allstate's 2025 America's Best Drivers Report, Metro Atlanta ranked 12th out of 200 U.S. cities for crash frequency, with Atlanta drivers 67 percent more likely to be in a collision than the national average. The Georgia Department of Transportation reports that 76 percent of crash fatalities in the state involve unsafe behaviors — distraction, impairment, or excessive speed. And a 2024 report from Propel ATL found that across five Metro Atlanta counties, traffic crash deaths (425) outnumbered homicides (410) for the year.

None of this is meant to alarm — it is meant to inform. If you drive in Metro Atlanta and carry only Georgia's minimum liability of 25/50/25, a single serious accident could leave you personally responsible for costs well above your policy limits. That is not a hypothetical. It happens regularly on Atlanta's roads, and it is exactly the kind of gap a coverage review is designed to find and address.

What Georgia's Minimum Actually Covers

Georgia requires $25,000 per person / $50,000 per accident / $25,000 property damage. A moderate accident involving medical care, lost wages, and vehicle damage can easily exceed those figures — leaving you personally liable for the difference.
