When to schedule a review

Most Reviews Start With One of These

You do not need a special reason to request a review. But if any of these applies, it is worth the conversation.

Your Rate Went Up

Georgia home insurance rates rose nearly 12% in the past year and auto rates have climbed sharply. If your renewal came in higher than expected and you did not receive a clear explanation, we review your policy, explain what is driving the increase, and walk through your options honestly.

You Bought a Home

Homeowners insurance placed at closing is often structured to satisfy the lender's minimum requirements — not to reflect your home's actual replacement cost or your liability exposure. A post-closing review checks dwelling limits, personal property coverage, and whether your policy has wind, hail, or separate storm deductibles you may not know about.

You Added a Teen Driver

Adding a teen driver is one of the most significant coverage changes a household makes. It affects your premium, your liability exposure, and which vehicle they are listed on. We review your current auto policy when you add a young driver and make sure your limits are appropriate — not just what Georgia requires.

Your Family Situation Changed

Marriage, divorce, a new child, or a family member moving in or out can all affect your coverage needs. Life insurance in particular should be reviewed any time your dependents or financial obligations change. We look at the full picture, not just one policy.

You Started or Bought a Business

Personal policies do not cover business activity. If you started working from home, purchased equipment, or launched a business, your existing home and auto policies may have gaps you are not aware of. We identify where personal coverage ends and where business coverage should begin.

You Haven't Reviewed in 2+ Years

Insurance needs change as life does. If your coverage has not been reviewed since you first set it up, there is a reasonable chance it no longer reflects your current situation, home value, or assets. A review takes 20 to 30 minutes and gives you clarity — even if nothing needs to change.

What a review covers

What Our Team Actually Looks At

A coverage review is not a sales call. It is a structured conversation in which we go through your current policies line by line and explain what each part covers — and what it does not. We do not assume you know what your deductibles are, what your liability limits mean in practice, or whether your dwelling coverage reflects what it would actually cost to rebuild your home today.

We tell you what we find. If your coverage is appropriate for your situation, we tell you that. If we identify something worth changing, we explain it clearly and let you decide. There is no pressure, no obligation, and nothing to sign.

Dwelling Coverage vs. Replacement Cost

We check whether your dwelling limit reflects what your home would actually cost to rebuild — not just its market value or original purchase price, both of which can differ significantly from rebuild cost.

Wind, Hail, and Storm Deductibles

Georgia insurers increasingly apply separate wind and hail deductibles that can reach 5% of your insured value. We identify whether you have one and what it means in a real claim scenario.

Auto Liability Limits

Georgia's minimum 25/50/25 requirement is a legal floor — not a recommendation. We review whether your current limits are appropriate for your assets and explain what happens when a claim exceeds your policy.

Uninsured Motorist Coverage

With 19% of Georgia drivers uninsured, this is one of the most important coverages on the road. We check whether you have it, at what limit, and whether it matches your auto liability coverage.

Life Insurance Coverage Gaps

If your life insurance was set up years ago and your income, mortgage, or family situation has changed, your coverage amount may no longer be adequate. We walk through what you have and whether it still fits.

Common Questions

Insurance Review — Questions Answered Directly

What is an insurance review?
A structured, no-charge conversation with a licensed agent who goes through your current home, auto, and life policies to check for coverage gaps, outdated limits, and anything that may have changed since your policy was last updated. It takes 20 to 30 minutes and requires no obligation to make any changes.
How much does an insurance review cost?
Nothing. Coverage reviews are provided at no charge to current and prospective clients. There is no obligation to make changes or purchase additional coverage as a result of the review.
Do I have to switch insurance companies after a review?
No. A review is informational. We tell you what we find honestly. If your current coverage is appropriate, we will tell you that. The decision to make any changes is always yours.
How long does a review take?
Most reviews take 20 to 30 minutes by phone. If you want to review all your policies in detail — home, auto, and life — it may take a bit longer. We can schedule a time that works for you or handle it in a single call at your convenience.
Can I do the review by phone or does it have to be in person?
Either works. Most clients prefer phone. If you would rather come in, our office is at 60 W Park St in Downtown Buford. We are open Monday through Friday 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM and Saturday by appointment.
I already have an agent. Can I still get a review?
Yes. You do not have to be a current client to request a review. Many people come to us after a rate increase or major life change and want an independent perspective on their coverage. Call 770-268-4418 and we will start from there.
