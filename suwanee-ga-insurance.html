Common Questions

Insurance Questions from Suwanee Residents

How close is your office to Suwanee?
Our office is at 60 W Park St in Downtown Buford — approximately 8 to 10 minutes from Suwanee Town Center. Most of our Suwanee clients handle everything by phone or email, but we are always available for in-person meetings by appointment.
My Suwanee home has gone up significantly in value. Is my coverage keeping up?
This is one of the most common issues we find during coverage reviews. Many Suwanee homeowners purchased their policies years ago when home values were lower. If your dwelling coverage has not been updated to reflect current rebuild costs, you may be significantly underinsured. We offer no-charge reviews to check this.
What does homeowners insurance typically not cover in Georgia?
Standard homeowners policies in Georgia generally do not cover flood damage, sewer backup, earthquake damage, or high-value personal property above category limits. We explain these gaps clearly and discuss whether additional coverage makes sense for your situation.
Does your agency handle auto insurance for Suwanee drivers?
Yes. Suwanee residents commuting on SR-20, I-985, or through the Lawrenceville-Suwanee Road corridor have real exposure on busy roads. We help Suwanee drivers review their liability limits, uninsured motorist coverage, and comprehensive and collision options.
What is an insurance review and is there a cost?
An insurance review is a conversation with one of our licensed agents where we go through your current coverage, identify any gaps or overlaps, and discuss whether your limits are appropriate for your situation. There is no charge for this service. Call 770-268-4418 or submit the form on this page to schedule one.
Do you offer life insurance for Suwanee families?
Yes. Life insurance is something many Suwanee families put off longer than they should. We help clients understand term versus permanent coverage, how much coverage is appropriate for their situation, and how life insurance fits into a broader financial plan.
