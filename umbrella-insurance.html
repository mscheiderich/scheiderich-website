How umbrella insurance works

Your Home and Auto Policies Have Liability Limits. Umbrella Coverage Extends Beyond Them.

Every liability policy has a ceiling — a maximum dollar amount it will pay on a single claim. When a serious accident, lawsuit, or liability judgment exceeds that ceiling, the balance becomes your personal financial responsibility. Your savings, your home equity, your wages — all potentially exposed.

Umbrella insurance is a personal liability policy that sits above your existing home, auto, motorcycle, boat, and other personal policies. When a claim exhausts the liability limits of an underlying policy, umbrella coverage picks up where it left off — typically in increments of one million dollars per occurrence.

It is called an umbrella because it extends across multiple underlying policies simultaneously. One umbrella policy covers the additional liability exposure from your home, your vehicles, your watercraft, and any other qualifying personal policies — in a single additional layer of protection.

Umbrella does not replace your underlying policies. It requires those policies to remain in place with minimum qualifying liability limits. When a claim exceeds those underlying limits, the umbrella activates.

Is it right for you?

Georgia Households That Benefit Most From Umbrella Coverage

Umbrella insurance is not only for the wealthy. It is for anyone whose personal assets — or future income — could be at risk if a serious liability claim exceeded their standard policy limits.

Homeowners With Significant Assets

Home equity, savings, and investment accounts are all subject to liability judgments in Georgia. If a serious accident on your property results in a judgment that exceeds your home liability limit, your personal assets are the next line of exposure.

Families With Teen Drivers

Teen drivers represent a statistically higher accident risk. A serious accident caused by a young driver can result in claims that quickly exceed standard auto liability limits. Umbrella coverage extends the protection for every driver on your policy.

Households With Pools or Trampolines

Pools and trampolines are among the most common sources of serious injury claims against homeowners. If a guest is severely injured, the resulting liability claim can be substantial. Umbrella coverage provides the additional layer that standard homeowners liability often cannot.

Dog Owners

Dog bite claims are one of the most frequent sources of homeowners liability claims in Georgia. The severity and resulting judgments can be significant. Umbrella coverage extends your liability protection for incidents involving pets beyond your home policy's limits.

Landlords

Rental property owners face liability exposure from tenants, guests, and the physical condition of their properties. An umbrella policy extends coverage above the liability limits of a landlord policy — protecting personal assets from claims arising at an investment property.

Boat and Watercraft Owners

Boating accidents on Georgia waterways can result in serious injury or fatality claims. If a watercraft liability limit is exhausted by a significant claim, umbrella coverage extends the protection — making it one of the most valuable add-ons for active boaters.

The current landscape

The Umbrella Insurance Market Has Shifted — Here Is What That Means for You

We believe in being straightforward about what is happening in the insurance market. This is an area where the landscape has changed and continues to evolve.

Umbrella Availability Has Tightened Industry-Wide

Over the past several years, the personal umbrella insurance market has changed significantly. Many carriers have tightened their underwriting guidelines, raised the minimum underlying liability limits required to qualify, or paused new umbrella policy issuance altogether. This is an industry-wide trend driven by rising litigation costs and large verdict awards.

What this means for you: umbrella insurance is still available to qualifying households in Georgia — but the qualification requirements are more stringent than they were a few years ago, and they vary by carrier. The minimum liability limits your underlying home and auto policies must carry before an umbrella will attach have increased at many carriers.

Common questions

Umbrella Insurance — Answered Plainly

What Georgia families most commonly ask about umbrella coverage.

What is umbrella insurance?
Umbrella insurance is a personal liability policy that sits above your existing home, auto, boat, motorcycle, and other personal policies. When a liability claim exhausts the limits of an underlying policy, umbrella coverage picks up where it left off — typically in increments of one million dollars per occurrence. It covers personal liability across all qualifying underlying policies in a single additional layer.
What does umbrella insurance NOT cover?
Umbrella insurance covers personal liability — it does not cover damage to your own property, your own medical expenses, or business-related liability. It also does not apply to intentional acts. Umbrella is specifically designed to extend the liability protection of your underlying personal policies when those limits are exhausted by a claim from a third party.
Does umbrella insurance require minimum underlying coverage?
Yes. Umbrella insurance requires your underlying home and auto policies to carry minimum liability limits before the umbrella will attach. These minimums vary by carrier and have increased at many carriers in recent years. The specific requirements that apply to your household depend on the carrier and current underwriting guidelines — which is why we recommend a direct conversation with our team for current information.
Is umbrella insurance worth it?
For households with meaningful assets, elevated liability exposure, or a lifestyle that includes risk factors like teen drivers, pools, dogs, boats, or rental properties — umbrella coverage provides significant financial protection for a relatively modest additional premium. The question is not whether you can afford umbrella insurance. It is whether you can afford to be without it if a serious claim exceeds your standard policy limits.
How do I know if I qualify for umbrella insurance?
Qualification depends on your underlying policy limits, your household profile, your claims history, and the carrier's current underwriting appetite. Because availability and requirements have been actively changing in the umbrella market, the most reliable way to find out what you qualify for is a direct conversation with our team. We will review your current policies and give you an honest, current answer.
